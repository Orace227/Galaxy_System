GSLV MK III

GSLV Mk-III is most powerfull rocket of ISRO!!!

GSLV Mk-III,chosen to launch Chandrayaan-2 spacecraft,is a three stage heavy launch vehicle developed by ISRO.The vehicle has two solid strapons,a core liquid booster and a cryogenic upper stage.

GSLV Mk-III is designed to carry 4 ton class of satellites into geosyncronous transfer Orbit (GTO) or about 10 ton to low earth Orbit(LEO),with is about twice the capablity GSLV Mk-III

The two strap-on motors of GSLV Mk-III are located on either side of its core liquid booster.Designted as 'S200' each carris 205 ton of composite solid propellant and their ignition results in vehicle lift-off.S200s function for 140 seconds. During strap-ons functioning phase,the two clustred Vikas liquid Engines of L100 liquid core booster will ignite of114 sec after lift-off to further augment the thrust of the vehicle.

characteristics of GSLV Mk-III

  • Performance capablity of 4.3 ton to GTO.
  • Cost effective.
  • Payload capablity to support 10 ton to Low Earth Orbit (LEO) missions.
  • Improved reliability, operability and redundancy management.
  • Future growth potential of payload with minimal design changes.
  • To support man rated missions of Indian Space Programme.

Details of GSLV MK-III

Payload to GTO:4,000 kg

GSLV Mk-III will be capable of placing the 4 tonne class satellites of the GSAT series into geosyncronous Transfer Orbit.

cryogenic Upper stage: C25

The c25 powered by CE-20,india's largest cryogenic Engine,designed and developed by the liquid propulison Systems centre.

Solid Rocket boosters: S200

GSLV Mk-III uses two S200 solid boosters to provide the huge amount of thrust requid for lift-off.The S200 was developed at vikram sarabhai Space centre.

vehicle Specification
Height: 43.43 m
vehicle Daimeter: 4.0 m
Heat Shield(Payload Fairing)Daimeter: 5.0 m
Number of Stage: 3
Lift off mass: 640 tonnes

Core stage: L110 Liuqid stage

The L110 liquid stage is powered by two vikas Engines designed and developed at the Liquid propulison Systems Centre.
